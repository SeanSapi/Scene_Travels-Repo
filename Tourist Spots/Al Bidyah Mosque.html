Al-Bidyah Mosque

About Al-Bidyah Mosque


The mud-brick Al-Bidyah Mosque, located 35 kilometers north of Fujairah city, is the UAE's oldest mosque and was named after the community that once surrounded it. Excavation of the area surrounding the mosque revealed that it had been inhabited for 4,000 years. A gigantic Iron Age tomb was uncovered in a big Islamic graveyard just to the north, revealing remnants of pottery, metal arrowheads, and other items going back to at least 1000 BC.

Activities


The small, square structure has an area of 53 square meters (570 sq ft) and was built from materials available in the area, primarily stones of various sizes and mud bricks coated in many layers of whitewashed plaster.


The roof has four squats, and helical domes that are supported by only one centrally placed pillar that also forms the ceiling. The entrance to the mosque is through double-winged wooden doors.


The prayer hall has a small mihrab (the niche in the wall that indicates the direction of Mecca), a simple pulpit, arches, and openings. A central pillar divides the internal space into four squares of similar dimensions. The pillar supports all four domes that can be seen from the exterior.


Inside the prayer hall, several small decorative windows allow light and air to enter the mosque. There are also cube-shaped spaces carved into the thick walls where copies of the Quran and other books are stored.
