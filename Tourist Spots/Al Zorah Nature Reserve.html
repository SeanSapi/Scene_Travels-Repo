Al-Zorah Nature Reserve

About


Originally an uninhabited sandy island surrounded by mangrove swamps, Al Zorah was disputed when, in 1866, Khalid bin Sultan Al Qasimi of Sharjah, supported by Sheikh Rashid bin Humaid Al Nuaimi II of Ajman and with the financial assistance of the Wahhabi agent, Turki bin Ahmed Al Sudairi, erected a fort there.

Activities


The thriving biodiversity of Al Zorah Nature Reserve is a welcome sanctuary for around 58 species of native and migrating birds. This ecotourism destination in Ajman, UAE is a great place for bird watchers to sight some egrets, herons and the pink flamingos among other avian populations.


Love yourself some beach time? You can have plenty of that here at this nature reserve in Ajman. Just pack a beach blanket and a wholesome picnic to enjoy with your friends and family. Laze around on the sand, build some sandcastles with your little ones or simply enjoy splashing around in the water.


Boating is the most popular activity for families at Al Zorah Natural Reserve, UAE. The facilities include paddle boats, rowing boats, and kayaks. Because of its popularity, it is common to find long waiting queues for boating at Al Zorah Nature Reserve. The best way to avoid the hassle is to go early in the morning or pre-book your slot.


Besides all these fun things to do at the Al Zorah Nature Reserve, you can also enjoy some laid-back tee time at the Al Zorah Golf Club located nearby. All you need to do is pre-book your playing time via the club’s website. The sessions are available from 06:00 am to 05:00 pm and are priced on a progressive basis, starting from AED 245.


One of the biggest tourist attractions in Al Zorah Natural Reserve is the mangrove tour. You can sign up for one of the guided mangrove kayaking tours that take you through the calm waters into the thick of the natural mangrove forests to explore the flora and fauna native to it. These kayaking tours are great for both expert and novice kayakers.
